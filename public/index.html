Understanding the Ark Protocol

What is Ark?

Important: Ark is a very new Bitcoin Layer 2 protocol. This wallet helps you use Ark without needing to know all the technical details.

Ark enables payments without having to create channels first.

Key Concepts

VTXOs (Virtual Transaction Outputs): Similar to Bitcoin's UTXOs, but they exist off-chain until needed on-chain.
Rounds: Collaborative processes where users can refresh their VTXOs.
Expiry Height: The block height at which a VTXO expires. The closer a VTXO gets to its expiry, the more important it is to refresh it.
Refresh: The process of extending a VTXO's lifetime and possibly changing its type (e.g., from "arkoor" to "round").

Common Actions

Receiving Funds: Share your VTXO pubkey with others. For testing, you can get free signet sats from the Ark faucet.
Sending Payments: You can send payments to other Ark users (via VTXO pubkey), to Lightning invoices, or to Bitcoin addresses.
Refreshing VTXOs: If your VTXOs are nearing expiry or you want to consolidate multiple VTXOs, use the "Refresh in Round" button.
Testing Your Wallet: Try buying something from the Ark Test Store!

Advanced Features

Boarding: Taking on-chain bitcoin off-chain, onto an Ark

This allows you to move your on-chain funds into the Ark protocol. You'll need to have an on-chain balance first. After boarding, you'll see a new VTXO with the type "board".

Command: bark board "10000 sat" or bark board --all

Unilateral Exit: Taking off-chain bitcoin on-chain, without any cooperation from the Ark server

This allows you to retrieve your sats on-chain if needed. The process involves broadcasting multiple transactions sequentially (takes 1-2 hours).

Requirements:

  • At least one VTXO
  • At least 15,000 on-chain sats per VTXO you're exiting
  • At least one confirmation for all on-chain UTXOs

Command: bark exit --vtxos <your_vtxo_id> --wait or bark exit --all --wait

For more detailed information, see the Advanced bark documentation.
